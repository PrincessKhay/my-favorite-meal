Pounded Yam & Egusi Soup

(My favorite meal)

ABOUT (origin)

Pounded yam is a traditional Nigerian dish made from boiled yams that are mashed into a smooth, sticky paste. Pounding the yams breaks down the complex carbohydrates into simple sugars, which makes pounded yam easier to digest than boiled yams. Pounded yam is also a good source of dietary fiber, which is important for digestive health. Pounded yam is typically served with a stew or sauce, or even soup like egusi soup, and can be eaten as a main course or side dish.
Pounded yam or Iy√°n(Yoruba) is a Nigerian food native to the Yoruba, Igbo, Ebira and Tiv ethnic groups. It is a traditional food. It is prepared by pounding boiled yam with a mortar and pestle. Pounded yam is similar to mashed potatoes but heavier. It is a smooth and tasty delicacy traditionally eaten with the hands.
It is popularly eaten with egusi soup, a tasty stew made from ground melon seeds, tomatoes, onion and red palm oil.
The type of yam used for pounding yam is the African yam which is common in Africa and some parts of Asia. It is also known as puna yam, true yam or white yam. The texture is rough with brown skin and off-white flesh; its length ranges from that of regular potatoes up to five feet long.
Be it any occasion, pounded yam and egusi soup never fails! It creates memories through its taste and flavours.

Ingredients and Measurements needed to prepare the meal

(For six servings)

INGREDIENTS MEASUREMENTS
Yam 2 tubers
Ground egusi seeds 1 pound
Beef, chicken, or fish (cooked) 1/2 pound
Onion (chopped) 2 medium size
Garlic (minced) 3 cloves
Tomatoes (chopped) 3
Ground ginger 1/2 teaspoon
Ground crayfish 1 spoon
Fresh red pepper 5
Red palm oil 1/2 cup
Seasoning cubes and salt Discrete amount
Water 2 cups (varies)
Chopped pumpkin leaves Desired amount

Method of Preparation

    For the pounded yam (using mortar and pestle):

  1. Use a sharp knife to peel the yam. Get rid of any discolorations or spots.
  2. Pour into the water and wash off all dirt immediately after cutting into large chunks.
  3. Using a medium pot, cook the yam under high heat with enough water to cover it.
  4. Boil for 20-30 minutes until tender. Drain yam thoroughly in a colander immediately after removing it from the heat.
  5. Keep the mortar and pestle clean by washing them. Pound the yam until smooth and stretchy in the mortar.
  6. When necessary, add water while pounding.
  7. Pound the yam some more till the water doesn't show.
  8. Roll the pounded yam into balls and wrap in a nylon to be served.

    For the egusi soup:

  1. In a large pot, heat the red palm oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions, garlic, and tomatoes, and cook until the vegetables are soft, then add the ground crayfish.
  3. Add the ground egusi seeds and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
  4. Add the water, beef, chicken, or fish, and bring to a boil.
  5. Add the chopped pumpkin leaves that has been washed thoroughly.
  6. Season the soup with salt and seasoning cubes to taste.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
